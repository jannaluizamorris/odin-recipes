Blueberry Pancakes

Recipe

My parents made blueberry pancakes for my brother and me every weekend when I was a kid. My mom grew a giant patch of blueberries in her garden and she would freeze so many of the berries that we could use them for pancakes year-round. They were so proud of how many berries they used; they used a lot of berries. When we had pancakes at restaurants they were outraged that each pancake had so few berries in it. Only eight blueberries in one pancake?! This is a disgrace! In this recipe, I invite you to disregard my parents' judgement and use as many or as few blueberries as your heart desires.

Ingredients

  • 1 cup Bob's Red Mill gluten-free pancake mix
  • 1 Tbsp melted salted butter
  • 1 large egg
  • 3/4 cup milk
  • fresh or frozen blueberries

Directions

  1. Heat a lightly greased griddle or pan over medium-high heat.
  2. Mix all ingredients together in a bowl until just combined. Do not over-mix.
  3. Pour 1/4 cup batter onto the hot pan.
  4. Quickly pour lots of (or a little) blueberries onto the batter and use a butterknife to spread the batter to cover the blueberries. This is so the berries don't stick to the pan after you flip the pancake.
  5. Cook until the batter bubbles, flip and let cook until done, about 2-3 minutes per side.
  6. Serve immediately. Makes 8 pancakes.
