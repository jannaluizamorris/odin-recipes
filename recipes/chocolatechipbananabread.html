Chocolate Chip Banana Bread

Recipe

This is one of my mom's favorite foods to bake and share with her kids. She and I have been gluten-free for almost ten years so we've been using gluten-free flour instead of white flour. I can honestly attest that I cannot taste the difference between the two different flours in this recipe. The bread is just as moist and light with gluten-free flour as it is with white flour. The addition of chocolate chips transforms banana bread from just ok to oops I seem to have eaten the entire loaf in an hour. Try it; I promise your taste buds will be so happy with you.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups Bob's Red Mill 1:1 gluten-free flour
  • 1/2 cup sugar
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 3 overripe bananas, mashed
  • 1/4 cup canola oil
  • 1/4 cup plain yogurt
  • 2 eggs, beaten with a fork
  • 1 tsp vanilla
  • 2/3 cup semisweet chocolate chips

Directions

  1. Sift dry ingredients together.
  2. Combine wet ingredients.
  3. Combine the dry and wet ingredients until just blended. Do not over-mix.
  4. Stir in chocolate chips.
  5. Generously grease bottom of a standard-size glass bread pan and pour in batter.
  6. Bake uncovered at 350 degrees for 50 minutes.
  7. Turn oven off and continue to bake for 5 more minutes.
  8. Cool on drying rack for 10 minutes, then loosen edges with a butter knife, remove from pan, and allow to finish cooling on the rack.
