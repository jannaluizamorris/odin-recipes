Rhubarb Cake

Recipe

This recipe is a childhood favorite. When the rhubarb cake is baking I can almost smell it in the kitchen of my childhood home. My favorite part is the cinnamon sugar topping. When the sugar melts in the oven, a crust forms that is somehow both crispy and melt-in-your-mouth buttery. If you bring this to a gathering, make sure to bring copies of the recipe because people will surely ask for it.

Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 cups brown sugar
  • 1/2 cup salted butter, softened, not melted
  • 2 eggs
  • 1 tsp baking soda
  • 1 cup buttermilk, or 1 cup milk + 1 Tbsp white vinegar
  • 2 cups flour or Bob's Red Mill 1:1 gluten-free flour
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 2 cups chopped rhubarb, add up to 2 extra cups for a more tart dessert
  • 1/3 cup sugar
  • 1 1/2 tsp cinnamon

Directions

  1. Cream brown sugar, butter, and eggs.
  2. In a separate bowl, mix baking soda and buttermilk together.
  3. In another separate bowl, mix flour and salt together.
  4. Alternate adding buttermilk mixture and flour mixture to creamed ingredients and mix gently.
  5. Fold in chopped rhubarb, then pour mixture into an ungreased 9x13 pan.
  6. Mix sugar and cinnamon together and evenly sprinkle on top of cake.
  7. Bake uncovered at 350 degrees for 40 minutes. Test doneness by inserting a toothpick; a dry toothpick means the cake is done.
  8. If the crust tastes too sweet or too spicy to you, experiment with changing the ratio of sugar to cinnamon next time you make the cake, which will probably be tomorrow.
